This examples show cases ploting simple bar plots using \texttt{pgfplots}.
To change a simple line plot to a bar plot add the \texttt{ybar} keyword to the \texttt{axis} configuration. 



\begin{figure}[h!]
  \centering
  \pgfkeys{/pgf/number format/.cd,1000 sep={\,}}  
  \begin{tikzpicture}
    \begin{axis}[
      grid=major,
      ybar,
      legend style={at={(0.5,-0.2)},anchor=north,legend columns=2},
      xlabel=Year,
      xtick=data,
      ymin=0
      ]
      \addplot [blue] table[x=Year, y=Number of students, col sep=comma] {../csvs/students.csv};
      \addplot [red] table[x=Year, y=Average grade, col sep=comma] {../csvs/students.csv};
      \legend{Number of students,Average grade}      
    \end{axis}
  \end{tikzpicture}
  \caption{Test of pgfplots}
  \label{fig:ladidad}
\end{figure}

The plot above can be hard to read as the bars are not filled, to change this add the \texttt{fill=<COLOR>} to \texttt{addplot} command and replac \texttt{<COLOR>} with your color of choice as shown here

\begin{verbatim}
\addplot [red, fill=red] table[x=Year, y=Average grade, col sep=comma]
     {../csvs/students.csv};} 
\end{verbatim}
